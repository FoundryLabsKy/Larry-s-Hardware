Change 01 · The strategy

A store that sells the visit — not an online shop.

✗ Before
A broken web-store

An online catalogue that competed with the real shop and mostly said “sold out.”

→
✓ After
A brochure that drives footfall

Hours, location and the reasons to come in today — no cart to break.

Why

Larry's wins in person, on price and convenience. A broken store hurt the brand more than no store would — so the site's one job is getting people through the door.

Change 02 · The homepage

A contact-first hero.

✗ Before
Products, no answers

No hours, no address, no phone — nothing a local needs before deciding to visit.

→
✓ After
The essentials, up top

Mon–Sat 8am–5pm, the Eastern Avenue address, and tap-to-call & WhatsApp buttons.

Why

Most visits start with a “near me” phone search, and people want the practical answers first. In Cayman, phone and WhatsApp are how customers actually reach a shop — so they're one tap away everywhere.

Change 03 · Products

Real departments, not dead SKUs.

✗ Before
A stale catalogue

Generic, mostly out-of-stock products that made a full store look empty.

→
✓ After
Ten aisles + “ask if we carry it”

Departments show the range; a light enquiry captures the “do you have X?” questions.

Why

Trying to track live stock is exactly what killed the old store. Showing the departments proves the breadth, while the enquiry captures real demand — and nothing on the page can go out of date.

Change 04 · The differentiator

The tyre shop, front and centre.

✗ Before
A hidden gem

Larry's Tyre & Battery — a genuine differentiator — appeared nowhere online.

→
✓ After
Its own page & a homepage band

“Get your tyres changed while you shop” — both locations, right up front.

Why

It's the one hardware run where you can sort the house and the car in a single stop. That's a real reason to choose Larry's over the big-box stores — so we made it impossible to miss.

Change 05 · Seasonality

Ready for storm season.

✗ Before
Silence

Nothing for the island's single biggest spike in seasonal demand.

→
✓ After
A hurricane-prep section

The practical checklist — tarps, water, batteries, lights — with “get it early” messaging.

Why

Cayman's June–November season drives sharp, high-intent demand for storm supplies. Being the site that answers that search — in season — is easy, well-timed traffic.

Change 06 · Getting found

One findable identity.

✗ Before
Scattered & conflicting

Three trade names, three postcodes, a 5pm/6pm hours clash, and no live .ky site.

→
✓ After
Consistent & structured

One canonical name, address & hours, plus HardwareStore data for Google.

Why

Local SEO decides who appears in “near me” results. Consistent details and a claimed Google Business Profile are the single biggest — and cheapest — levers for showing up.

Change 07 · Look & voice

An honest, signature look.

✗ Before
A generic template

An off-the-shelf store theme selling snorkels and shampoo — nothing that said “Larry's.”

→
✓ After
Shop-signage identity

Navy, hardware red, warm sand, condensed type and a hang-tag motif — with real reviews.

Why

Three in four people judge a business by its website. A distinctive, honest look builds trust — and where facts weren't public, like the founder's story, we left space rather than inventing it.

The through-line

What changed — and what didn't.

What changed

From an abandoned web-store into a fast, findable, honest brochure that leads with hours, location and one-tap contact — and puts the tyre shop and storm-prep where people can see them.

What stayed the same

The store itself: affordable, family-owned, one-stop, since 1986. The site just finally tells that story — and helps the right people find it.
